# Abstract

Academic procrastination remains a significant challenge affecting student performance and well-being in higher education institutions. Traditional approaches often lack personalized, timely intervention strategies that respect ethical boundaries while providing effective support. This project presents PRCI v2.0, an Adaptive AI-Based Procrastination Prediction & Personalized Intervention System designed to address academic procrastination through ethical AI technology within appropriate educational boundaries.

PRCI v2.0 implements a comprehensive system architecture integrating classical machine learning for text analysis, rule-based risk assessment, and template-driven intervention generation. The system processes student academic text inputs through TF-IDF vectorization and logistic regression models to detect procrastination indicators and root causes. Risk assessment utilizes rule-based aggregation to determine procrastination risk levels without clinical inference. Interventions are generated through ethical template matching and personalized through explicit user feedback, maintaining non-clinical boundaries throughout all interactions.

The methodology emphasizes ethical AI design with strict separation from clinical or therapeutic applications. The system employs rule-based logic for risk assessment to ensure transparency and explainability, while using classical ML approaches for text processing to maintain simplicity and interpretability. Personalization occurs through voluntary user feedback only, avoiding behavioral profiling or surveillance. The system includes comprehensive safety frameworks, boundary enforcement mechanisms, and professional resource referrals when appropriate.

Evaluation focuses on system reliability, consistency, and appropriateness rather than clinical effectiveness, with conservative interpretation of results within academic support contexts. The system demonstrates consistent behavior across test scenarios while maintaining appropriate ethical boundaries and professional standards.

**The system is designed for academic support and does not provide psychological or medical diagnosis.** PRCI v2.0 contributes to educational technology by demonstrating ethical AI implementation for academic procrastination support while maintaining appropriate professional boundaries and user autonomy. The project provides a foundation for responsible AI applications in educational settings with clear limitations and appropriate scope.

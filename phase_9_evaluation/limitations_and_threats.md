# Limitations and Threats to Validity: PRCI v2.0

## Overview

This document provides honest and conservative assessment of PRCI v2.0's limitations, data constraints, rule-based constraints, and threats to validity. The analysis emphasizes the need for conservative interpretation of results and appropriate generalization boundaries.

## Data Limitations

### Academic Context Constraints

**Limited Academic Diversity**:
- System developed and tested primarily with traditional university student scenarios
- Limited exposure to diverse educational contexts and institutions
- Minimal validation with non-traditional academic programs or students
- Conservative generalization to broader academic populations

**Cultural and Linguistic Boundaries**:
- Development focused on English-language academic contexts
- Limited validation with diverse cultural backgrounds and expressions
- Minimal testing with non-Western educational approaches and terminology
- Potential cultural bias in intervention generation and language use

**Disciplinary Scope Limitations**:
- Primary focus on general academic procrastination patterns
- Limited specialization for specific academic disciplines or fields
- Minimal validation with professional or graduate-level academic challenges
- Conservative applicability to specialized academic contexts

### Sample Size and Diversity Constraints

**Limited User Population**:
- Testing conducted with small, convenience sample of users
- Minimal demographic diversity in validation testing
- Limited representation of different academic backgrounds and experiences
- Conservative generalization to broader student populations

**Academic Level Specificity**:
- Primary development for undergraduate university students
- Limited validation with graduate or professional students
- Minimal testing with non-traditional or returning students
- Conservative applicability across different academic levels and experiences

## Rule-Based Constraints

### Logic Rigidity Limitations

**Inflexible Rule Application**:
- System applies rules rigidly without contextual nuance
- Limited ability to adapt to unique or unusual academic situations
- Conservative approach may miss appropriate intervention opportunities
- Potential over-simplification of complex procrastination scenarios

**Binary Decision Boundaries**:
- Risk assessment uses discrete categories (LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH)
- Limited granularity in risk level differentiation and response
- Conservative thresholds may misclassify borderline academic situations
- Potential loss of nuance in intervention selection and personalization

**Limited Learning Capability**:
- Rule-based system cannot learn from new patterns or experiences
- Minimal adaptation to evolving academic challenges and contexts
- Conservative approach may become outdated over time
- Limited ability to improve from user feedback and interactions

### Intervention Generation Constraints

**Template-Based Limitations**:
- Interventions generated from predefined templates and patterns
- Limited creativity or adaptation to unique user situations
- Conservative approach may reduce intervention relevance and impact
- Potential repetition or predictability in intervention content

**Scope Boundary Enforcement**:
- Strict academic support boundaries may limit intervention effectiveness
- Conservative approach may avoid helpful but borderline suggestions
- Limited ability to address complex multi-factorial procrastination causes
- Potential under-support for serious academic challenges

- Potential inconsistency in evaluation across different assessors

**Cultural Bias in Evaluation**:

- Limited consideration of diverse educational perspectives and approaches

- Potential undervaluation of culturally-specific intervention approaches

The limitations and threats analysis reveals significant constraints on PRCI v2.0's generalizability and effectiveness. **Why results should not be generalized**:
- Limited testing scope and population diversity constrain generalizability

The analysis supports conservative interpretation of system effectiveness and appropriate generalization boundaries for academic procrastination support technology.

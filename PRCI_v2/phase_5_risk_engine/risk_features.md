# Risk Features: PRCI v2.0

## Overview

This document outlines the conceptual features used in procrastination risk assessment. These features are derived from aggregated detection results and focus on temporal patterns, behavioral trends, and short-term risk indicators while maintaining academic context and non-clinical boundaries.

## Emotional Intensity Trends

### Anxiety Signal Accumulation

**Pattern Description**
- Increasing frequency of anxiety-like signals over time
- Growing intensity of stress indicators in recent detection results
- Escalating pattern of academic worry and concern expressions
- Temporal acceleration of anxiety-related language use

**Academic Context Indicators**
- Rising anxiety related to upcoming deadlines and exams
- Increasing stress signals during high-pressure academic periods
- Pattern of intensifying anxiety around major assignments
- Temporal clustering of anxiety indicators during challenging coursework

**Risk Assessment Value**
- Trending anxiety suggests increasing procrastination vulnerability
- Accelerating anxiety patterns may indicate approaching crisis points
- Consistent anxiety accumulation signals chronic stress factors
- Pattern intensity guides intervention urgency and resource allocation

### Depression Signal Persistence

**Pattern Description**
- Sustained presence of low motivation and energy indicators
- Persistent expression of helplessness and overwhelm in detection results
- Continuing pattern of academic disengagement and withdrawal
- Ongoing presence of negative self-talk and defeatist language

**Academic Context Indicators**
- Persistent low motivation during extended academic periods
- Sustained negative self-assessment across multiple assignments
- Continuing pattern of academic avoidance and disengagement
- Persistent expression of academic inadequacy concerns

**Risk Assessment Value**
- Sustained negative indicators suggest entrenched procrastination patterns
- Persistent depression-like signals may indicate need for comprehensive support
- Continuing negative patterns may require intervention escalation
- Duration and intensity guide support level and intervention type

### Emotional Volatility

**Pattern Description**
- Rapid fluctuations between emotional states in detection results
- Inconsistent pattern of anxiety and motivation levels
- Frequent emotional state changes without clear triggers
- Unstable emotional responses to academic challenges

**Academic Context Indicators**
- Emotional ups and downs related to academic performance variations
- Inconsistent responses to similar academic challenges
- Variable emotional reactions to different types of assignments
- Fluctuating confidence levels across academic activities

**Risk Assessment Value**
- High emotional volatility may indicate difficulty coping with academic stress
- Inconsistent patterns may suggest poor emotional regulation strategies
- Volatile responses may require stabilization and coping skill development
- Pattern variability guides intervention focus on emotional regulation

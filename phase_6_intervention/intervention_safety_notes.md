# Intervention Safety Notes: PRCI v2.0

## Overview

This document outlines the safety framework and ethical boundaries for PRCI v2.0's intervention engine. The approach prioritizes user safety, appropriate boundaries, and responsible intervention delivery while maintaining academic focus and behavioral support orientation.

## Explicit Non-Clinical Disclaimer

### System Purpose Clarification

**Academic Support Focus**
- PRCI v2.0 is designed specifically for academic procrastination support
- Interventions focus on behavioral strategies and productivity enhancement
- System provides educational and skill-building support only
- No therapeutic, clinical, or medical services are provided

**Boundary Definition**
- Interventions are supportive suggestions, not professional advice
- System does not provide diagnosis, treatment, or therapy
- No clinical assessment or mental health services are offered
- Clear separation from professional counseling and medical services

**User Understanding**
- Users must understand system limitations and boundaries
- Interventions supplement, not replace, professional services
- Users maintain full autonomy and decision-making control
- System serves as educational support tool only

### Disclaimer Implementation

**Clear Communication**
- All interventions include appropriate disclaimers and boundary statements
- Users are regularly reminded of system limitations and scope
- Professional service referrals are provided when appropriate
- Emergency resources are available for crisis situations

**Informed Consent**
- Users receive clear information about system capabilities and limitations
- Intervention purposes and boundaries are explicitly stated
- Users understand they can seek professional help at any time
- System use is voluntary and user-controlled

## No Emergency Handling

### System Limitations

**Crisis Response Boundaries**
- PRCI v2.0 is not equipped to handle emergency situations
- No crisis intervention or emergency response capabilities are provided
- System cannot provide immediate help for urgent mental health needs
- Emergency situations require professional crisis services

**Appropriate Resource Referral**
- Clear guidance to emergency and crisis resources when needed
- Direct connection to professional crisis intervention services
- Immediate referral pathways for urgent mental health needs
- Emergency contact information and resource directories

**User Responsibility**
- Users must seek appropriate professional help for emergency situations
- System use does not replace emergency services or crisis intervention
- Users encouraged to maintain awareness of professional resource availability
- Clear understanding of when to seek emergency professional help

### Emergency Protocol

**Detection and Response**
- System identifies potential emergency situations through user inputs
- Immediate referral to appropriate emergency resources and services
- Clear guidance on accessing professional crisis intervention
- Support for users in accessing emergency help when needed

**Resource Integration**
- Integration with university emergency and crisis services
- Connection to national and local crisis intervention resources
- Clear pathways to professional emergency mental health services
- Support for users in navigating emergency resource systems

## No Authority Framing

### User Autonomy Preservation

**Suggestion-Based Approach**
- All interventions are presented as suggestions, not commands
- Users maintain complete control over intervention acceptance and implementation
- No authoritative or prescriptive language is used
- User choice and preference are respected throughout system interaction

**Collaborative Orientation**
- System serves as supportive partner in academic success
- Interventions offered as options for consideration and exploration
- User expertise and experience are valued and incorporated
- Collaborative approach respects user knowledge and autonomy

**Empowerment Focus**
- Interventions designed to enhance user capabilities and self-efficacy
- Support for user decision-making and problem-solving skills
- Emphasis on user strengths and existing successful strategies
- Empowering language that supports user agency and control

### Language and Tone Guidelines

**Non-Authoritative Communication**
- Avoidance of commanding or directive language patterns
- Use of suggestive and supportive communication styles
- Respect for user expertise and personal circumstances
- Collaborative and partnership-oriented language

**Respectful Interaction**
- Recognition of user as expert in their own experience
- Respect for personal values, preferences, and circumstances
- Non-judgmental approach to user challenges and difficulties
- Supportive and encouraging communication throughout interactions

**Boundary Maintenance**
- Clear distinction between support and professional advice
- Avoidance of diagnostic or therapeutic language
- Respect for professional boundaries and limitations
- Appropriate referral to professional services when needed

The safety framework ensures that PRCI v2.0 maintains appropriate academic focus while providing valuable behavioral support that complements, rather than replaces, professional services and human expertise.
